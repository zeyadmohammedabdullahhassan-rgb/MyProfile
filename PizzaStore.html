Pizza Sales Data Analysis

End-to-End Data Science Project using Python & Power BI

01 Data Loading & Initial Exploration

The dataset was imported into Python using Pandas. After loading the Excel file, we performed an initial inspection using df.head() to understand the structure, column names, and sample records before starting the cleaning process.

02 Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

We inspected data types and missing values, converted date and time columns into proper datetime format, and extracted new time-based features such as day name and order hour to support further sales trend analysis.

03 Sales Trend Analysis

We calculated key business metrics including total revenue, total orders, total pizzas sold, average order value, and average pizzas per order. Additionally, we analyzed sales trends by day of the week and by hour to identify peak business periods and customer purchasing patterns.

04 Product Performance Analysis

We analyzed product performance by evaluating revenue distribution across pizza categories and sizes. We also identified the top 5 best-selling and bottom 5 worst-selling pizzas based on total quantity sold to understand product demand and optimize inventory decisions.

05 Sales Distribution by Category

We analyzed the total number of pizzas sold by category using grouped aggregation. The bar chart shows the distribution of sales volume across categories, highlighting that the Classic category achieved the highest total units sold compared to other categories.

06 Business Dashboard & Insights

A comprehensive Power BI dashboard was developed to present key metrics and insights in an interactive format. The dashboard includes revenue KPIs, daily and hourly sales trends, category and size distribution, and best/worst-selling products. This enables decision-makers to quickly evaluate performance and identify growth opportunities.
